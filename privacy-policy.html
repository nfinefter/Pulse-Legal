Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 9, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Nicholas Finefter (“we,” “us,” or “our”) collects, uses, and shares your information when you use Pulse (the “Service” or “Pulse App”). Pulse is a tool that helps content creators view and consolidate income and earnings data from their linked social media and creator platforms.

The Service includes our website and the Pulse mobile application.

1. Information We Collect

We collect the following categories of information:

  • Account Information: Email address and other details you provide when registering or signing in (including data from third-party authentication providers).
  • Linked Platform Data: When you choose to connect third-party creator platforms, we receive and store the data those platforms make available through their authorized APIs or login flows. This may include:
    • Account identifiers, usernames, display names, profile avatars, and channel IDs
    • Income, earnings, revenue, payout metrics, subscription data, or other monetization information (where the platform provides it and you grant permission)

    Currently supported platforms include (but are not limited to):

    • Twitch
    • YouTube
    • TikTok
    • OnlyFans
    • Kick
    • Rumble
    • Instagram
    • Snapchat
  • Usage and Technical Data: Device type, IP address (for approximate location and security), browser/app version, log timestamps, and diagnostic information needed to operate and secure the Service.
  • User-Provided Content: Any notes, manual entries, or additional data you add within Pulse.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information to:

  • Provide, maintain, and improve the Pulse Service, including displaying your consolidated earnings dashboard;
  • Authenticate you and securely link the creator platforms you authorize;
  • Fetch, process, and display income-related data from your connected accounts (e.g., Twitch subscriptions, YouTube AdSense, TikTok Creator Fund, OnlyFans earnings, Kick payouts, etc.);
  • Send important service communications (security alerts, updates, or support messages);
  • Detect, prevent, and address fraud, abuse, or technical problems;
  • Comply with legal and regulatory obligations.

3. How We Share Your Information

We may share your information with:

  • Service Providers: Vendors that help us with hosting, cloud storage, authentication, analytics, or other operational functions. These providers are contractually required to safeguard your data.
  • Linked Platforms: When you connect platforms such as Twitch, YouTube, TikTok, OnlyFans, Kick, Rumble, Instagram, or Snapchat, those companies process your data according to their own privacy policies and terms. We only request the minimum permissions needed for the features you enable.
  • Legal Requirements: When required by law, subpoena, court order, or to protect the rights, safety, or property of Pulse, our users, or the public.
  • Business Transfers: In the context of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, with appropriate notice where required.

We do not sell your personal information as that term is defined under California or other U.S. state privacy laws. We do not use your data for targeted advertising on third-party platforms unless we first obtain your consent where legally required.

4. Platform-Specific Notes

TikTok: Authorization is handled via TikTok’s Login Kit or similar flow and is subject to TikTok’s Privacy Policy. We do not receive or store your TikTok password and only access data you explicitly authorize.

For all other platforms (Twitch, YouTube, OnlyFans, Kick, Rumble, Instagram, Snapchat, etc.), data access is governed by each platform’s respective terms, privacy policy, and API permissions. Pulse only receives the information the platform returns after you grant consent.

5. Data Storage, Security, and Retention

We use reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect your information. Data may be processed and stored in the United States or other countries where our service providers operate.

We retain your information only as long as necessary to provide the Service, fulfill the purposes described here, or comply with legal requirements.

6. Cookies and Similar Technologies

We and our providers may use cookies, local storage, and similar technologies for authentication, security, functionality, and performance. You can manage these through your browser or device settings.

7. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on where you live (including California under the CCPA/CPRA), you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or port your personal data, or to opt out of certain processing or sales/sharing of data. To exercise these rights, contact us at nfinefter@gmail.com.

We will verify your identity before responding. You may also contact your local data protection authority if you have concerns.

8. Children’s Privacy

Pulse is not intended for children under 13 (or the minimum age required in your jurisdiction). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe we have done so, please contact us immediately.

9. International Users

If you are accessing Pulse from outside the United States, your information may be transferred to and processed in the U.S. and other countries that may have different data protection standards.

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this policy periodically. We will post the revised version here and update the “Last updated” date. For significant changes, we will provide additional notice where required by law.
